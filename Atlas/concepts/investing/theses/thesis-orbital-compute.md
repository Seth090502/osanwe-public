# Thesis: Orbital Compute -- DEMO

> **DEMO CONTENT.** This thesis is deliberately fictional -- a worked example
> showing the essay structure the framework expects. Every figure below is
> synthetic. Replace with your own theses authored from `_templates/thesis.md`.

## Thesis statement

Latency-tolerant batch compute (model training, rendering, archival
analytics) migrates to orbital data centers over the next decade as launch
cost per kg falls below the terrestrial cost of land + cooling + grid
interconnection in supply-constrained metros. Operators who own
radiation-hardened compute capacity and optical downlink rights capture a
disproportionate share of the new capacity layer.

## Key metrics to track

- Launch cost per kg to LEO (target curve: synthetic $850/kg by 2030)
- On-orbit compute capacity announcements (MW-equivalent)
- Optical downlink throughput milestones (Tbps per ground station)
- Terrestrial data-center interconnection queue length (the constraint this
  thesis arbitrages)
- Radiation-hardening cost premium vs terrestrial silicon (synthetic: 2.1x,
  falling ~15%/yr in the demo model)

## Amber signals (early warning)

1. Launch cost-curve flattens for 4+ consecutive quarters
2. A major terrestrial grid-interconnection reform bill passes (removes the
   constraint being arbitraged)
3. On-orbit failure rate above 2x the demo model's assumption
4. Optical downlink licensing stalls at the regulator

## Invalidation trigger

Two consecutive years of launch-cost-per-kg DECLINE STALL combined with a
terrestrial interconnection-queue clearing trend -- the cost crossover never
arrives and the thesis premise (orbital cheaper than constrained terrestrial)
fails. On invalidation: mandatory `/decide` review; exit ladder per
[[doctrine.template]] Section 3.
